A Local's Guide to Exploring Assam

Assam is not just a destination; it's an emotion. From the roar of the Rhino to the tranquility of the Brahmaputra, here is exactly how you should experience the land of the Red River and Blue Hills.

The Wildlife Experience

Assam is the last stronghold of the magnificent One-Horned Rhinoceros. A trip to Assam is incomplete without witnessing these gentle giants in their natural habitat in Kaziranga National Park.

Beyond Rhinos, keep your eyes peeled for Wild Water Buffaloes, Swamp Deer, and the elusive Royal Bengal Tiger.

Pro Tips:

  • Book an Elephant Safari for early morning (5:30 AM).
  • Jeep Safaris are best in the afternoon (1:30 PM).
  • Best time to visit: November to April.

Culture & Heritage

Assam is a melting pot of cultures. Start your spiritual journey at the ancient Kamakhya Temple in Guwahati. Travel deeper to Sivasagar to see the architectural marvels of the Ahom Kingdom.

Must Try:

  • Taste the authentic 'Masor Tenga' (Sour Fish Curry).
  • Buy traditional Muga Silk (Golden Silk).
  • Visit Srimanta Sankaradeva Kalakshetra in Guwahati.

River, Islands & Tea

The Brahmaputra is the lifeline of Assam. Take a ferry to Majuli, the world's largest river island. No trip is complete without exploring the lush green tea gardens of Jorhat or Dibrugarh.

Experiences:

  • Sunset River Cruise on the Brahmaputra.
  • Stay in a Bamboo Hut in Majuli.
  • Stay in a colonial-era Chang Bungalow.
